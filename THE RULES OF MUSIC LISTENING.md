# **THE RULES OF MUSIC LISTENING**

_Abstract: This set of rules evolved over the course of almost 10 years in order to allow for the listening of the greatest amount of good music. It tries to balance discovery movements (new artists, new albums and new tracks by known artists) and recovery movements (listening again to known artists and enjoying deeply the best of what's been discovered). These rules are live and meant to be adjusted (as they have been) to better allow for the objectives. _

# **1\. GENERAL DEFINITIONS**

1. One month is counted as 4 weeks. This makes the year 12 months (48 weeks) \+ 4 free weeks (that should account for vacations and other non-listening time).
2. Listening weeks start on Friday and end the next Thursday, to match [last.fm](http://last.fm)'s counter. Usually, weekend time is counted as Friday and/or free time.
3. A liked song cannot be skipped, so a song should be liked when it's expected to be enjoyable to listen in its entirety at any moment. It should be unliked if it's meant to be skipped.
4. Keeping the library clean and organized is important to make the lists function properly. As the library is rather huge, this has to be done piecewise, usually when artists pages are accessed for any reason. Overall, one should save only an artist's studio full length albums in their plain versions. Exceptions are:
    1. albums that are part of the most popular,
    2. live albums that have been otherwise recommended,
    3. EPs and singles by bands that don't have a lot of full length albums,
    4. deluxe versions by very liked bands (over 1000 scrobbles).
5. If a track isn't liked, check if the album is either saved, or added to a monthly set. In case it isn't, check if the artist is being followed and have at least 4 albums saved (or as part of a monthly set). In case it isn't, follow the artist and add to "the queue" and/or add the necessary albums to complete 4\.
6. Whenever there’s a reference below to “randomly picking a date and select something from”, refer to these rules:
    1. Access [random.org/calendar-dates](http://random.org/calendar-dates) to generate dates
    2. Pick dates between 2007-11-27 and the cut off date:
        1. For artists in discography “memory lane”: Dec 31st of the previous year
        2. For albums in album “memory lane”: Dec 31st of the previous year
        3. For tracks in Friday Routine: Dec 31st of 5 years prior
    3. Generate the dates
    4. Open the date in [last.fm](http://last.fm) library:
        1. For artists in discography “memory lane”: choose “Artists” by most scrobbles
        2. For albums in album “memory lane”: choose “Albums” by most scrobbles
        3. For tracks in Friday Routine: choose scrobbles by time of play
    5. Check the timestamp of generation:
        1. The last digit of the minutes decide the direction of checking the list items:
            1. 0-4 \-\> top down
            2. 5-9 \-\> bottom up
        2. The seconds tell the position of the selected item

# **2\. MAIN LIST**

The main list is monthly created following the alphabetical order of saved albums. This generates a monthly set, which tracks the albums that have been listened to already. This is the original form of the project \- all other lists have been created to get around the sheer size of this endeavor.

## **RULES:**

1. Every monthly set should be comprised of 250 albums. This should sum up to 3000 albums per year, being completed in 40 years (2065) at 120000 albums listened.
2. For every album, a verdict between "remove" and "keep" should be given.
    1. In general, the criteria for keeping an album is having at least half of the tracks liked.
    2. Unavailable or repeated albums should be always removed.
    3. Albums added to a monthly set and then removed should not be added again permanently unless at least half of its tracks have been liked.
    4. On average, a Pareto distribution is expected, with 80% of albums being removed and 20% being kept.
3. After certain thresholds of liked songs, there are some actions that should be taken:
    1. **After 3 liked songs**: add all (non-removed) albums by the artist. This is also a good time to adjust the library for this artist.
    2. **After 6 liked songs**: add artist to "the queue" track list. The album now should be kept irrespective of other criteria.
    3. **After 9 liked songs**: add artist to "newfoundland" discography list
4. In general, 3 songs from each album should be scrobbled. If there are less than 3 tracks, the whole album should be scrobbled. If any of the tracks is liked, one should go through (though not necessarily scrobble) the next 3 tracks. After that, the rest of the album can be skipped.

# **3\. DISCOGRAPHIES**

Discographies are a way of diving deeper into artists, observing their development over time. This is the first deviation around the main project, and originally only comprised discographies that were saved to hard disk. Now it encompasses artists that were recently discovered, as well as old friends.

## **RULES:**

1. Every month, there's space for 40 discography albums.
2. Discography artists should be picked from the following lists:
    1. "newfoundland", which contains artists stemming from the main list (and other discovery methods). Most of these will be new artists, but some are known artists. In the latter case, they should only be added here if the discography has never been played in full (for all practical reasons, this is equivalent to having less than 500 scrobbles)
    2. "memory lane", which contains artists whose discographies have already been played. This playlist is filled in one of two forms:
        1. The top 5 artists of the previous year (added to the top, not the bottom, of the list).
        2. If the list is empty, by randomly picking a date and selecting an artist listened to on that day.
    3. "the requeue", which contains artists whose discographies have already been played (or have more than 500 scrobbles) and which aren't in the "memory lane" list.
3. The 40 slots should be filled by picking the first of each list, in the order listed above, until either all slots have been filled or the next artist won't fit in the available slots. In the latter case, the next list should be perused for an artists that will fit. If there are none, then proceed in cycle to the next list until one is found. In the (unlikely case) where none fit, then the slots can be left open.
4. If any lists aren't visited because of a lack of slots, then in the following month, they should be the first.
5. Once a discography has been listened to, an extra list (see later) of the "Actually, this is..." type can be created, in special if the artists has an extensive discography (over 20 albums) but only relatively few liked tracks. These will stay in the "compilations" folder. Also, if there are many non-album singles, a playlist for those can be created as well.

# **4\. ALBUM LISTS**

Album lists were a second deviation from the main project, aimed at allowing the discovery of newly released albums, as well as (re)discovering older albums. The lists vary in their rotation speed and depth of listening, which is meant to allow perusing a rather vast selection of albums.

## **RULES**

1. There are five album lists, but they can be understood as four independent sets:
    1. A seasonal set ("memory lane" and "it looks like a playlist but it's a reminder"), which are filled and emptied as follows:
        1. In January of each year, "memory lane" is filled with the top 20 albums of the previous year. These are all listened within that month.
        2. In February, March, September, October, November and December of each year, 20 albums are listened per month from the "it looks like a playlist but it's a reminder" list. This sums up to 120 albums. During these months, no albums can be added to either list.
        3. In April, May, June, July and August of each year, 10 albums are consumed from each list. "memory lane" is filled at the beginning of each month by randomly picking 10 dates and selecting one album from each. Adding albums to "it looks like a playlist but it's a reminder" is free until it reaches 120 \+ (10 \* RM) albums, where RM is the amount of months left until September.
    2. A rotational set ("cutting in from of the line bc we're special").
        1. The list is meant to be consumed in one single month, at a rate of 40 albums per month.
        2. Albums can be freely be added until the 40 album cap.
        3. Rotating through some artists albums is expected.
    3. A devotional set ("should spend more time with these guys" and "my several best friends"), which contains albums to be listened on repeat.
        1. Albums should be consumed at a rate of one per week
        2. 10 repeats per track (5 times full album \+ 5 times each track).
        3. Albums are added from either discographies or from other album lists, as long as  at least 80% of the tracks have been liked.
        4. Changes in the order can be expected to put albums by the same artist back to back.
        5. Once the albums have been listened to in "should spend more time with these guys", they are permanently moved to "my several best friends". In the (unlikely) event of "should spend more time with these guys" being empty, all albums can be moved back from "my several best friends". Otherwise, the only case where one album can be moved from "my several best friends" to "should spend more time with these guys" is after listening to the discography of an artist.
        6. This list that doesn't allow for other content to be played on top.
    4. A discovery set ("new vintage"), which contains new albums by top 50 scrobbled bands, to be listened on repeat for a little time:
        1. Albums should be consumed in order of release date, and should only be removed once the cycle is done
        2. Number of repeats depends on position in top 50 at time of release:
            1. 1-5: 10 scrobbles every day for a week for singles, 2 runs every day for 4 weeks for albums
            2. 5-10: 5 scrobbles every day for a week for singles, 1 run every day for 4 weeks for albums
            3. 10-20: 1 scrobbles every day for a week for singles, 1 run every day for 2 weeks for albums
            4. 20-50: 1 run every day for 1 weeks for albums only
        3. This is all in addition to other lists

# **5\. TRACK LISTS**

Track lists have evolved from the need to allow single tracks to be listened without depending on listening to the whole album. They grew in number to specialize in each of the needs they cater to (discovery, recovery, deepening, etc).

## **RULES**

1. The main track list is "daily mind radio", which contains tracks that pop up in the mind from time to time, and sometimes stick there.
    1. Tracks should be added only when they spontaneously pop in the mind (so, not when they've been suggested by other people singing, PA playing it, etc).
    2. Tracks should be added once per each time they pop in the mind. Since Spotify has made it difficult to add the same track to the same playlist, "staging" playlists have been created to track the repetitions.
    3. Tracks should be played on the following day to their being added to the list.
    4. In case they have been added several times, tracks should be repeated twice, and left at the bottom of the playlist to be played on the next day.
    5. In case listening days are skipped and tracks accumulate, they should be all listened to on the next listening day.
    6. Tracks should never be played on the day they are added to avoid infinite loops.
2. Then there's "slow listening", which contain tracks to be investigated in depth.
    1. Only artists with more than 1000 scrobbles can be added here. Add always the first track of the discography.
    2. The list has a cap of 10 tracks, i.e., 10 artists being explored.
    3. Tracks should be repeated 5 times before moving on.
    4. After listening to a track, the next one in the discography should be added at the bottom.
    5. Only once a discography is finished can the last track be removed.
3. Then there's an ecosystem of discovery and a recovery playlists:

    1. The recovery playlists:

        1. "new wine from old bottles" contains tracks from the latest album by artists with at least 100 scrobbles.
            1. Each track is listened to once and the next track in the album is added at the end of the playlist.
            2. In case of upcoming releases, follow the tracking list (if available) or the release order of the singles.
            3. There's a 10 track cap.
            4. Tracks should be added from the top of "wine cellar" unless it's empty.
        2. "wine cellar" contains the tracks that should have gone into "new wine from old bottles" but haven't because of the track cap. This list is never played directly.
        3. "a blast from the past" contains tracks that have been listened to before. They can come into the playlist in some ways:

            1. when they have been suggested by outside sources (other people singing, playing in PA, etc)
            2. as part of the Friday Routines (see below)
            3. as part of the Saturday Routines (see below), if they haven't been listened to this season
            4. as part of the Monday Routines (see below)

            This playlist has a 200 track cap, at which point it enters "flush mode". In this mode, 10 tracks have to be removed every day for a month, and no new tracks can be added.

    2. The discovery playlists:

        1. "found art" contains never before heard tracks. They can come into the playlist in some ways:

            1. when they have been recommended by outside sources (social media posts, other people, bands playing live, etc)
            2. as part of the Friday Routines (see below)
            3. as part of the Saturday Routines (see below)

            This playlist has a 200 track cap, at which point it enters "flush mode". In this mode, 10 tracks have to be removed every day for a month, and no new tracks can be added. If bands discovered here are specially good, they can be added to "the queue".

        2. "the queue" contains tracks by artists waiting to be discovered. These artists can come into the playlist in some ways:

            1. From the main list, after 6 likes
            2. When visiting an artist's page, for any reason, if they have at least 6 liked tracks (except when visiting from the “found art” list).
            3. From "found art", if the artist is really good.
            4. From album lists, if the album is really good.
            5. From the Friday Routines (see below)
            6. From extra lists, if the artist is really good.

            The playing rules for this playlist mimic those of "new kids on the block", as it serves as a backup for the tracks that couldn't go there yet because of the track cap. While this playlist can be played (unlike "wine cellar"), it mostly won't and will lay dormant until its tracks are pulled by "new kids on the block".
            Very important: **never add artists whose discography you already listened to, or that have more than 50 liked songs**

        3. "new kids on the block" contains tracks stemming from "the queue".
            1. Each track is listened to once and the next track from the artist is added at the end of the playlist.
            2. For each artist, play the top 10 most popular tracks. If at least 6 are liked, then play the top 4 most popular albums.
            3. After finishing an artist, if there are at least 18 liked tracks, add them to the year's "Great Discoveries" playlist. The artist can also be kept in play with other lists.
            4. There's a 10 track cap.
            5. Tracks should be added from the top of "the queue" unless it's empty.

# **6\. EXTRA LISTS**

Extra lists aren't really part of the project, but can be fitted in into available time to enhance discovery (and also possibly recovery) in otherwise unthought of ways.

## **RULES**

1. There are fundamentally three types of extra lists: setlists, compilations and pre-made lists.
    1. Setlists are created for shows which I've been to, when available. These can be played if there are available time.
    2. Compilations are created to gather tracks for a certain motive. Currently, there are six types of compilations:
        1. "Actually, this is..." lists, which are compiled after listening to an artist's discography, if it's very extensive. These help when coming back to these artists without needing to laboriously listen to 20+ records to get to a few good tracks. These can be played if there's available time.
        2. "For People" lists, which are compiled to share music with other people. These in general aren't listened.
        3. "Great Discoveries" lists, which are compiled every year from the artists coming out of "new kids on the block", if they have at least 18 likes. While they won't be played directly, every new year, the great discoveries of the previous year should be put back in play using the other lists. This should also be done after 5 years (so, in 2030, put the 2025 Great Discoveries back in play, etc).
        4. "Non-album Singles" lists, which are compiled when listening to an artist's discography, if it has many non-album singles. This allows for all of these to be played as a single album, reducing the count of slots an artist takes.
        5. "Production Discographies" lists, which are compiled if members of very dear artists (over 5000 scrobbles) are also producers. These then should be played as part of these artist's discographies.
        6. "Unavailable Albums" lists, which compile available songs from albums currently not available on Spotify, so that they can be played in the proper order.
    3. Pre-made lists are thematic lists made by other people, by myself or by Spotify. These are preferentially played when there's available time.
2. Every year, a few charts are also produced: top 20 albums and top 50 tracks. These are all played in January of the next year.
3. Besides these types, there's also the "Liked Songs" playlist, which should be played in random if there are no other playlists to fill available time.

## **RULES**

1\. There are 4 free weeks in the year. Use these to cover non-listening time, such as vacations, while keeping the objectives.  
2\. In case there are more than 4 weeks of non-listening time, Week 4 can be omitted (with the proper adjustments to the discographies slots) if needed.  
3\. Extra lists should be selected from the "next up" folder. If none fits 5h, then use setlists and compilations. If there none left, pre-made playlists can be tackled piecewise in separate months, possibly by stringing together the 5h from each month together (week 4 flows into week 1 of next month, introducing a little delay that will be tackled in the next week 4). Alternatively, note down the last track and continue the next month.

## **ROUTINES**

Routines are regular checking of sources to add to certain playlists to aid in discovery and recovery movements. There are three:

### **Friday Routine**

1. Open the "Music" tab from [last.fm](http://last.fm).
2. Open "Artists for You", "New releases to check out", "Albums you might like", "Tracks you need to hear", and "The latest releases"
3. In "Artists for You":
    1. Add the first artists to "the queue". In case it's already added, add the second. In case both are already added, don't do anything.
    2. Follow and save the necessary 4 albums for each new artists, and add them to “the queue”
4. In “New releases to check out”:
    1. Check releases from the previous Friday up to the current one
    2. Add albums by artists with at least 100 scrobbles to the “wine cellar” (or directly to “new wine from old bottles” if “wine cellar” is empty and there are available slots)
    3. If there’s available slots, add albums to the albums lists (either the rotational or seasonal set). Else, add the first track of the album to “found art” (unless it’s in flush mode).
5. In “Albums you might like”:
    1. Check the first page only
    2. If there’s available slots, add albums to the albums lists (either the rotational or seasonal set). Else, add the first track of the album to “found art” (unless it’s in flush mode).
6. In “Tracks you need to hear”:
    1. Check the first page only
    2. Add up to 10 tracks to “found art” (unless it’s in flush mode)
7. In “The latest releases”:
    1. Check releases from the previous Friday up to the current one
    2. Add albums by artists with at least 100 scrobbles to the “wine cellar” (or directly to “new wine from old bottles” if “wine cellar” is empty and there are available slots)
    3. If there’s available slots, add albums to the albums lists (either the rotational or seasonal set). Else, add the first track of the album to “found art” (unless it’s in flush mode).
8. Randomly pick 10 dates and select one track from each to add to “a blast from the past”

### **Saturday Routine**

1. In Spotify, check the “Check” folder
2. Go through all the lists there, adding tracks
    1. Add to “a blast from the past” if tracks haven’t been listened in this season but are known
    2. Add to “new wine from old bottles” (or “wine cellar”) if tracks have recently been released by artists with over 100 scrobbles
    3. Add to “found art” if tracks are new and don’t fit in the above lists

### **Monday Routines**

1. In [lastfmstats.com](http://lastfmstats.com) go to the “Golden oldies” section
2. Open all the artists that can be found in Spotify
3. Choose a year (try not to repeat between iterations) and add up to 5 of the most scrobbled tracks to “a blast from the past”
