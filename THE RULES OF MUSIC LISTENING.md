# **THE RULES OF MUSIC LISTENING**

_Abstract: This set of rules evolved over the course of almost 10 years in order to allow for the listening of the greatest amount of good music. It tries to balance discovery movements (new artists, new albums and new tracks by known artists) and recovery movements (listening again to known artists and enjoying deeply the best of what's been discovered). These rules are live and meant to be adjusted (as they have been) to better allow for the objectives._

# **1\. GENERAL DEFINITIONS**

1. One month is counted as 4 weeks. This makes the year 12 months (48 weeks) \+ 4 free weeks (that should account for vacations and other non-listening time).
2. Listening weeks start on Friday and end the next Thursday, to match [last.fm](http://last.fm)'s counter. Usually, weekend time is counted as Friday and/or free time.
3. A liked song cannot be skipped, so a song should be liked when it's expected to be enjoyable to listen in its entirety at any moment. It should be unliked if it's meant to be skipped.
4. Keeping the library clean and organized is important to make the lists function properly. As the library is rather huge, this has to be done piecewise, usually when artists pages are accessed for any reason. Overall, one should save only an artist's studio full length albums in their plain versions. Exceptions are:
    1. albums that are part of the most popular,
    2. live albums that have been otherwise recommended,
    3. EPs and singles by bands that don't have a lot of full length albums,
    4. deluxe versions by very liked bands (over 1000 scrobbles).
5. If a track isn't liked, check if the album already meets the saving criteria (>50% liked tracks). In case it isn't, check if the artist is being followed. In case it isn't, follow the artist.
6. Whenever there’s a reference below to “randomly picking a date and select something from”, refer to these rules:
    1. Access [random.org/calendar-dates](http://random.org/calendar-dates) to generate dates
    2. Pick dates between 2007-11-27 and the cut off date:
        1. For artists in discography “memory lane”: Dec 31st of the previous year
        2. For albums in album “palace of memory”: Dec 31st of the previous year
        3. For tracks in Friday Routine: Dec 31st of 5 years prior
    3. Generate the dates
    4. Open the date in [last.fm](http://last.fm) library:
        1. For artists in discography “memory lane”: choose “Artists” by most scrobbles
        2. For albums in album “palace of memory”: choose “Albums” by most scrobbles
        3. For tracks in Friday Routine: choose scrobbles by time of play
    5. Check the timestamp of generation:
        1. The first digit of the minutes decides the page to check:
           1. 0 = 6
           2. If there are less pages than what's needed, wrap around
           3. If there's only one page, there's only one page
        2. The last digit of the minutes decide the direction of checking the list items:
            1. 0-4 \-\> top down
            2. 5-9 \-\> bottom up
        3. The seconds tell the position of the selected item

# **3\. TRACK LISTS**

Track lists have evolved from the need to allow single tracks to be listened without depending on listening to the whole album. They grew in number to specialize in each of the needs they cater to (discovery, recovery, deepening, etc).

1. *a blast from the past* contains tracks that have been listened to before.
   1. **Type**: RECOVERY
   2. **Fill-up**:
       1. as part of the Friday Routines (see below)
       2. as part of the Saturday Routines (see below), if they haven't been listened to this season
       3. as part of the Monday Routines (see below)
    3. **Flush**: 10/day (15/day when Monday Routines apply)
    4. **Cap**: 50/week (75/week when Monday Routines apply)
2. *daily mind radio* contains tracks that pop up in the mind from time to time, and sometimes stick there
   1. **Type**: RECOVERY
   2. **Fill-up**: 
      1. When tracks spontaneously pop in the mind, once per each time they pop in the mind. Since Spotify has made it difficult to add the same track to the same playlist, "staging" playlists have been created to track the repetitions. In case they have been added several times, tracks should be repeated twice, and left at the bottom of the playlist to be played on the next day.
      2. Every day, randomly picking a datetime and select one track from the same day last year, and every 5 years prior to that (currently: 2025, 2020, 2015, 2010)
   3. **Flush**:
      1. Tracks should be played on the following day to their being added to the list, then removed.
      2. In case listening days are skipped and tracks accumulate, they should be all listened to on the next listening day.
3. *found art* contains never before heard tracks
   1. **Type**: DISCOVERY
   2. **Fill-up**:
      1. when tracks have been recommended by outside sources (social media posts, other people, bands playing live, etc)
      2. as part of the Friday Routines (see below)
      3. as part of the Saturday Routines (see below)
   3. **Flush**:
      1. 10/day (more up to 15/day when there are late additions)
      2. if liked, the track's artist should be added to *the queue*
   4. **Cap**: 50/week
4. *genre reveal* contains tracks from genre-based playlists from [Every Noise at Once](https://everynoise.com/)
   1. **Type**: DISCOVERY
   2. **Fill-up**: 10/day, coming from the next genre in line (we're going bottom-up, left-right)
   3. **Flush**: 10/day, liked tracks' artists should be added to *the queue*
   4. **Cap**: 10/day, trivially
5. *new kids on the block* contains tracks from artists under discovery, coming from *the queue 2*
   1. **Type**: DISCOVERY
   2. **Fill-up**: Top of *the queue 2*, when there's a spot
   3. **Flush**:
      1. Listen each track and add the next track from the artist's top albums at the end of the playlist.
      2. Follow the top albums by track.
      3. Skip album after 3 unliked tracks
      4. Play 4 albums
      5. After finishing an artist, add them to the year's *Great Discoveries* playlist and to *newfoundland* discography if they meet at least one these criteria:
         1. 18 liked tracks
         2. 3 saved releases
         3. All releases saved
         4. All tracks liked
      6. Otherwise, unfollow the artist and add the top liked track to *Unlucky Ones*
   4. **Cap**: 10 tracks (10 listened/day)
6. *new wine from old bottles* containts tracks from the latest releases that came under radar
   1. **Type**: DISCOVERY
   2. **Fill-up**: Top of *wine cellar*, when there's a spot
   3. **Flush**:
      1. Listen each track and add the next track from the artist at the end of the playlist.
      2. Follow the release by track. In case of upcoming releases, follow the tracking list (if available) or the release order of the singles.
      3. Skip album after 3 unliked tracks
      4. After reaching the last track of an album or EP (but not singles), add it to *sauvignon terre-neuve* album list
   4. **Cap**: 10 tracks (10 listened/day)
7. *the queue* contains tracks by artists waiting to be discovered
   1. **Type**: DISCOVERY
   2. **Fill-up**:
      1. From *found art*, *genre reveal*, *something old, something new*, *sauvignon terre-neuve* (or really anywhere), when an artist has its first like
      2. From the Friday Routines (see below)
   3. **Flush**:
      1. Normally:
         1. Play artist's top songs and add the next top track. (Pay attention to add only tracks from the first artist)
         2. When you reach the end, if there's 5 liked tracks, add top album's first track to *the queue 2*
         3. Otherwise, unfollow artist and add top liked track to *Unlucky Ones*
         4. If 6 liked songs is reached before the end, add top album's first track to *the queue 2*
      2. When cap is reached:
         1. Play artist's 10 top songs straight and make decision to promote to *the queue 2* or unfollow immediately.
         2. Do 20 artists per day for 3 months (60 listening days)
         3. All other DISCOVERY playlists are stopped during this flush
         4. Return to normal once you reach half of the cap
   4. **Cap**: 2400 tracks (10 listened/day normally, 200/day on flush)
8. *the queue 2: attack of the albums* is the continuation of *the queue* on to artists' top albums
   1. **Type**: DISCOVERY
   2. **Fill-up**: From *the queue* (or really anywhere) when artist reaches 5/10 or 6 liked tracks
   3. **Flush**:
      1. Normally: same as *new kids on the block*, flushing into that list when spots are available
      2. When cap is reached:
         1. Play all 4 top albums of the artist straight and make decision to save to *Great Discoveries* or *Unlucky Ones*
         2. Do 5 artists per day for 3 months (60 listening days)
         3. All other DISCOVERY playlists are stooped during this flush
         4. Return to normal once you reach 3/4 of the cap
   4. **Cap**: 1200 tracks (10 listened/day normally, ~200/day on flush)
9. *the queue 3: revenge of these artists* contains tracks by artists that have been fully discovered and are awaiting to have their full discograpy listend to
   1.  **Type**: HYBRID (RECOVERY/DISCOVERY)
   2.  **Fill-up**:
       1.  From previous year's *Great Discoveries* at the beginning of the current year
       2.  When an artist has more than 18 liked songs
   3.  **Flush**:
       1.  Listen each track and add the next track from the artist at the end of the playlist
       2.  Follow the albums by track
       3.  Play the whole discography chronologically
       4.  Remove from list when discography is taken up in *newfoundland*
   4.  **Cap**: No cap, 10 listened/day
10. *slow listening* contains tracks to be investigated in depth
    1.  **Type**: RECOVERY
    2.  **Fill-up**: Artists that you decide to investigate in depth
    3.  **Flush**:
        1.  Same as *the queue 3*, but play each track 5 times before moving on.
        2.  Only remove when reach the last track of the last album.
    4.  **Cap**:
        1.  10 tracks (2 listened/day, 10/week)
11. *something old, something new* contains either tracks from artist from the "Golden Oldies" [lastfmstats.com](http://lastfmstats.com) or is a timeslot to follow the followed artists list alphabetically
    1.  **Type**: ALTERNATING (RECOVERY/DISCOVERY) -> do one day of each
    2.  **Fill-up**:
        1.  For *something old*:
            1.  If the list is empty, head to [lastfmstats.com](http://lastfmstats.com) and open the top artist
            2.  Choose an album or a group of 10 tracks by the artist and add
        2.  For *something new*: Play the next artist's top tracks in the followed artists alphabetical list
    1.  **Flush**:
        1.  For *something old*: Remove track after being played
        2.  For *something new*: If liked one track by artist, add to the *the queue*, unless it's only one album and there are slots in *sauvignon terre-neuve*, then add there. If not liked the first 3 top tracks, unfollow artist.
    2.  **Cap**:
        1.  For *something old*: 10 tracks or 1 album, ~10 listend/day
        2.  For *something new*: no cap, as much as time allows
12. *wine cellar* contains the tracks that should have gone into "new wine from old bottles" but haven't because of the track cap. This list is never played directly.
    1.  **Type**: UNPLAYED/DISCOVERY
    2.  **Fill-up**:
        1.  From Friday Routines (see below)
        2.  From Saturday Routines (see below)
        3.  Anytime you hear about a new record
    3.  **Flush**: Into *new wine from old bottles*, when there are free slots

# **4\. ALBUM LISTS**

Album lists were a second deviation from the main project, aimed at allowing the discovery of newly released albums, as well as (re)discovering older albums. The lists vary in their rotation speed and depth of listening, which is meant to allow perusing a rather vast selection of albums.

1. *should spend more time with these guys* contains albums to be listened on repeat
   1. **Type**: RECOVERY
   2. **Fill-up**: From discographies, when more than 80% of tracks are liked
   3. **Flush**:
      1. 1 album/week
      2. 10 repeats per track (5 times full album \+ 5 times each track).
      3. Once the albums have been listened to in "should spend more time with these guys", they are permanently moved to "my several best friends". In the (unlikely) event of "should spend more time with these guys" being empty, all albums can be moved back from "my several best friends". Otherwise, the only case where one album can be moved from "my several best friends" to "should spend more time with these guys" is after relistening to the discography of an artist in *the requeue*.
2. *sauvignon terre-neuve* contains new albums to be discovered
   1. **Type**: DISCOVERY
   2. **Fill-up**:
      1. From *new wine from old bottles*, in case the last track is reached
      2. From *something old, something new* when the artist only has one release and there are free slots
      3. From Friday Routines (see below), if there are available slots
   3. **Flush**:
      1. 1 album/day, 5/week
      2. May trigger moving artists from *the queue* to *the queue 2*
   4. **Cap**: 20 albums (1 month of listening)
3. *requeue for a dream* contains albums from artists you already have listened to their discography
   1. **Type**: RECOVERY
   2. **Fill-up**: From artists in *the requeue*, in case you want to speed up listening to them
   3. **Flush**: 1 album/day, 5/week
   4. **Cap** 20 albums (but usually kept at 10, always at multiples of 5)
4. *palace of memory* contains albums hailing from history (of scrobbles)
   1. **Type**: RECOVERY
   2. **Fill-up**:
      1. From Friday Routines (see below)
      2. From *a blast from the past* or *daily mind radio*, if you really want to listed to the album
   3. **Flush**: 1 album/day, 5/week
   4. **Cap**: 20 albums (but usually kept at 5, or some multiple thereof)
5. *new vintage* contains newly released albums by your top 50 artitsts
   1. **Type**: DISCOVERY
   2. **Fill-up**: Whenever an artist in yout top 50 on last.fm (at the date of release), releases something new
   3. **Flush**:
      1. Albums should be consumed in order of release date, and should only be removed once the cycle is done
      2. Number of repeats depends on position in top 50 at time of release:
            1. 1-5: 10 scrobbles every day for a week for singles, 2 runs every day for 4 weeks for albums
            2. 5-10: 5 scrobbles every day for a week for singles, 1 run every day for 4 weeks for albums
            3. 10-20: 1 scrobble every day for a week for singles, 1 run every day for 2 weeks for albums
            4. 20-50: 1 run every day for 1 week for albums only
      3. In the (not uncommon) case that his list is empty, use the time to play some of the extra lists

# **5\. DISCOGRAPHIES**

Discographies are a way of diving deeper into artists, observing their development over time. This is the first deviation around the main project, and originally only comprised discographies that were saved to hard disk. Now it encompasses artists that were recently discovered, as well as old friends.

1. Every week, there's space for 10 discography albums, 2/day.
2. You should try to get artists to fill a round week (so the sum of albums should be multiple of 10)
3. Discography artists should be picked from the following lists:
    1. *newfoundland*, which contains artists stemming from the DISCOVERY lists. Most of these will be new artists, but some are known artists. In the latter case, they should only be added here if the discography has never been played in full.
    2. *memory lane*, which contains artists whose discographies have already been played. This playlist is filled in one of two forms:
        1. The top 5 artists of the previous year (added to the top, not the bottom, of the list).
        2. If the list is empty, by randomly picking a date and selecting an artist listened to on that day.
    3. *the requeue*, which contains artists whose discographies have already been played and which aren't in the *memory lane* list.
4. The slots should be filled by picking the first of each list, in the order listed above, until either you reach a multiple of 10. In case that can't be done, the next list should be perused for an artists that will fit. If there are none, then proceed in cycle to the next list until one is found. In the (unlikely case) where none fit, then the slots can be left open.
5. If any lists aren't visited because of a lack of slots, then in the following month, they should be the first.
6. Once a discography has been listened to, an extra list (see later) of the "Actually, this is..." type can be created, in special if the artists has an extensive discography (over 20 albums) but only relatively few liked tracks. These will stay in the "compilations" folder. Also, if there are many non-album singles, a playlist for those can be created as well.
7. Albums with more than 80% of tracks liked should be added to *should spend more time with these guys*.


# **6\. EXTRA LISTS**

Extra lists aren't really part of the project, but can be fitted in into available time to enhance discovery (and also possibly recovery) in otherwise unthought of ways.

## **RULES**

1. There are fundamentally three types of extra lists: setlists, compilations and pre-made lists.
    1. Setlists are created for shows which I've been to, when available. These can be played if there are available time.
    2. Compilations are created to gather tracks for a certain motive. Currently, there are six types of compilations:
        1. "Actually, this is..." lists, which are compiled after listening to an artist's discography, if it's very extensive. These help when coming back to these artists without needing to laboriously listen to 20+ records to get to a few good tracks. These can be played if there's available time.
        2. "For People" lists, which are compiled to share music with other people. These in general aren't listened.
        3. "Great Discoveries" lists, which are compiled every year from the artists coming out of "new kids on the block", if they have at least 18 likes. While they won't be played directly, every new year, the great discoveries of the previous year should be put back in play using the other lists. This should also be done after 5 years (so, in 2030, put the 2025 Great Discoveries back in play, etc).
        4. "Non-album Singles" lists, which are compiled when listening to an artist's discography, if it has many non-album singles. This allows for all of these to be played as a single album, reducing the count of slots an artist takes.
        5. "Production Discographies" lists, which are compiled if members of very dear artists (over 5000 scrobbles) are also producers. These then should be played as part of these artist's discographies.
        6. "Unavailable Albums" lists, which compile available songs from albums currently not available on Spotify, so that they can be played in the proper order.
    3. Pre-made lists are thematic lists made by other people, by myself or by Spotify. These are preferentially played when there's available time.
2. Every year, a few charts are also produced: top 20 albums and top 50 tracks. These are all played in January of the next year.
3. Besides these types, there's also the "Liked Songs" playlist, which should be played in random if there are no other playlists to fill available time.

## **ROUTINES**

Routines are regular checking of sources to add to certain playlists to aid in discovery and recovery movements. There are three:

### **Friday Routine**

1. Open the "Music" tab from [last.fm](http://last.fm).
2. Open "Artists for You", "New releases to check out", "Albums you might like", "Tracks you need to hear", and "The latest releases"
3. In "Artists for You":
    1. Add all artists to *the queue*.
4. In “New releases to check out”:
    1. Check releases from the previous Friday up to the current one
    2. Add albums you want to listen to *wine cellar* (or directly to *new wine from old bottles*, if *wine cellar* is empty and there are available slots)
5. In “Albums you might like”:
    1. Check the first page only
    2. If there’s available slots, add albums to the *sauvignon terre-neuve*.
6. In “Tracks you need to hear”:
    1. Add 20 tracks to *found art*
7. In “The latest releases”:
    1. Check releases from the previous Friday up to the current one
    2. Add albums you want to listen to *wine cellar* (or directly to *new wine from old bottles* if *wine cellar* is empty and there are available slots)
8. Randomly pick dates and select one track from each to add to *a blast from the past* until it reaches 10 tracks.
9. Randomly pick dates and select one album from each to add to *palace of memory* until it reaches 5 albums.

### **Saturday Routine**

1. In Spotify, check the “Check” folder
2. Go through the "Blend" lists and add:
   1. to *a blast from the past* if tracks haven’t been listened in this season but are known
   2. to *found art* if tracks are new (up to 10 tracks)
3. Go through the "Discovery Weekly" list and add 20 tracks to *found art*. Add more if there's still less than 40 afterwards
4. Fo through "Release Radar" and add interesting new releases to *wine cellar*

### **Monday Routines**

1. In [lastfmstats.com](http://lastfmstats.com) go to the "Every year artists" and add all missing ones from 5 years prior to a list
2. Over the weeks, add 5/day to *a blast from the past*
